1. Time (Czas)

In Polish, telling time varies depending on whether you are being Casual (everyday conversation) or Formal (schedules, news, radio).

Formal Style: Uses a 24-hour clock. Hours are spoken as Feminine Ordinal numbers (e.g., pierwsza, trzynasta), and minutes are spoken as standard cardinal numbers.

Example: 13:50 is "Trzynasta piƒôƒádziesiƒÖt".

Casual Style: Uses a 12-hour clock and focuses on the "next hour."

  • On the hour: Uses the Nominative Feminine form (e.g., 2:00 is druga).
  • Half-past: Uses the phrase "wp√≥≈Ç do" followed by the Genitive of the next hour.
    Example: 2:30 is "wp√≥≈Ç do trzeciej" (halfway to the third).
  • Minutes past: Uses the number of minutes followed by "po" and the Genitive of the current hour.
    Example: 2:10 is "dziesiƒôƒá po drugiej".
  • Minutes to: Uses "za" followed by the number of minutes and the Nominative of the next hour.
    Example: 1:50 is "za dziesiƒôƒá druga".
2. Dates (Daty)

Dates in Polish require switching between the Nominative (naming the day) and the Genitive (stating when something happens).

  • The "Naming" Rule (Nominative): Used for phrases like "Today is..." (Dzisiaj jest...). Both the day and the month change:
    • The Day: Use a Masculine Ordinal number (e.g., pierwszy, drugi, trzeci).
    • The Month: Use the Genitive form of the month name (e.g., stycznia, lutego).
    • Example: May 5th is "PiƒÖty maja".
  • The "Event" Rule (Genitive): Used for phrases like "On the 5th of May..." (PiƒÖtego maja...).
    • The Day: The ordinal number switches to the Genitive ending in -ego.
    • Example: On May 5th is "PiƒÖtego maja".
  • The Year: When stating a year, the number is followed by the word "roku" (Genitive of rok). The year itself is also spoken in its Genitive form.
üöª Gender: 1 & 2

Only 1 and 2 change to match gender:

1: jeden (m), jedna (f), jedno (n).

2: dwa (m/n), dwie (f).

3. General Rule for Nouns: 1, 2-4, 5+

When counting things (like hours or days), the ending of the noun changes based on the number:

  • 1: Singular (e.g., 1 godzina).
  • 2, 3, 4: Nominative Plural (e.g., 3 godziny).
  • 5 or more: Genitive Plural (e.g., 5 godzin).

Note: Numbers ending in 2, 3, or 4 (like 22, 23, 24) follow the "2-4" rule, except for the "teens" (12, 13, 14), which always follow the "5+" Genitive rule.
